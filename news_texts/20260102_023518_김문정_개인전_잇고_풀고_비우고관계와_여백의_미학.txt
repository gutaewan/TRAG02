The Aesthetics of Relationships and Margin Pursuing the Aesthetics of Relationships Blossoming in Margins Break News So Jung Han reporter = Kim Moon-jung's solo exhibition, "Connect, Untie, Empty," to be held at Space Pusup in Jongno-gu, Seoul, from January 10 to 17, 2026, is expected to go beyond a mere presentation of artwork and establish itself as a significant forum for contemplating "relationships" and "time" in Korean contemporary art. Kim Moon-jung has consistently explored the traces of time and the structure of relationships. In her previous works, she revealed the material properties of materials and visualized the layers of relationships through overlap and margins. This exhibition represents a deeper exploration of this exploration, using the sculptural language of interdependence and interpenetration, grounded in the philosophy of dependent origination (緣起思想). The intertwining and interconnectedness of the work, the gaps revealed through unraveling, and the sense of connection becoming clearer through emptiness go beyond mere formative experimentation; they are an attempt to visually reveal relational ontology. This also aligns with "relational aesthetics," a key topic in contemporary art discourse. The moment viewers encounter the work, they create new relationships through their own experiences and memories, and the exhibition itself is recreated and connected as a living world. Kim Moon-jung's work is recognized as a new interpretation of the aesthetics of emptiness in Korean contemporary art. The emptiness is not simply a void, but rather a space where relationships are created and transformed. This expands the context of traditional Korean aesthetics into a contemporary context, while establishing itself as a unique language for contemplating "relationships" and "time" within global art discourse. The artist states, "I hope you will observe together how the structure of relationships is formed and transformed," and hopes that viewers will discover their own unique connections within the work.
