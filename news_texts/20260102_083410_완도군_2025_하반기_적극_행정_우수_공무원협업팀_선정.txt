(Wando=Break News) Reporter Lee Hak-soo = Wando County, South Jeolla Province, announced the selection of four civil servants and two collaborative teams for the "Proactive Administration in the Second Half of 2025," who have contributed to improving the quality of life for residents through creative and professional work. Wando County announced that it held a Proactive Administration Committee meeting on December 24th and finalized the selection process from a total of nine outstanding examples through online voting and presentations. In this evaluation, Lee Hye-in, a senior officer in the Planning and Budget Office, was recognized for her innovative approach to operating "Hope Wando," the county's official social media channel, and for effectively communicating information about marine healing and tourism. Hwang Cheol-woong, team leader of the Administrative Support Division, established a "big data and AI-based aquaculture platform" to predict natural disasters such as high water temperatures and establish a foundation for stabilizing abalone prices, earning a favorable response from fishermen. Moreover, Moon Byeong-min, team leader of the Tax Accounting Division, secured 230 million won worth of public property free of charge, which can be utilized as a future wind power plant. Ahn A-ran, a senior citizen officer in Geumil-eup, operated a "Mobile Happiness Civil Service Center" for senior citizens, providing customized administrative services. In the collaboration category, the Administrative Support Division's General Affairs Team and the Safety Management and Social Disaster Team were jointly selected. They boldly abolished the existing inefficient on-duty system and implemented a 24-hour system with specialized personnel. This initiative not only reduced employee fatigue, but also secured the golden hour for disaster safety and minimized administrative gaps. Excellent public officials selected for this award are provided with exceptional benefits. The county plans to encourage proactive administrative practices within the public service by offering personnel incentives such as bonuses, one-grade performance bonuses, performance-based bonuses, vacations, transfers to desired departments, and overseas training opportunities. Lee Beom-woo, Chairman of the Proactive Administrative Committee (Vice County Mayor), emphasized, "Flexible and creative work practices that break free from convention lead to the happiness of the county residents.
